How the Jio Token Network works under the hood

The Jio Token Network is a vertically integrated AI fabric that runs on top of our 4G/5G and fibre assets, combining AI-optimised data centres, edge computing, models, and orchestration into a single token-metered platform.

A seven-layer intelligence stack

Jio is building a seven-layer architecture covering infrastructure, compute, AI platforms, telecom integration, edge intelligence, applications and governance.

Layer What it includes Token relevance
1 Physical & Power National fibre, 4G/5G, metro & hyperscale DCs, renewables Low-latency, in-country AI compute
2 Compute & Chips GPUs, accelerators, indigenous AI chip efforts Cost-efficient token generation at scale
3 Core AI Platforms JioBrain, Jio AI Cloud Training/serving LLMs & SLMs, token metering
4 Open Telecom AI With AMD, Cisco, Nokia LLM-agnostic, agentic AI across 4G/5G core, RAN, OSS/BSS
5 Edge Intelligence 5G MEC, COs, home/enterprise CPE Split inference, low-latency tokens at the edge
6 AI Applications Consumer, enterprise, network AIOps agents Token-consuming workloads
7 Orchestration Policy, routing, billing, safety Token allocation, QoS, compliance

From Packet Flows to Token Flows

Today we optimise for bytes, sessions and QoS. With the Jio Token Network, we add a new dimension: token flows tied to AI workloads.

Network functions (5G core, UPF, MEC platforms) route AI traffic to dedicated AI edges where models are hosted, and our AI platform meters tokens consumed per interaction, user, app and partner.

This allows us to expose differentiated services – for example, low-latency agent rails, AI-rich slices or off-peak AI pricing – while still integrating cleanly with our existing OSS/BSS.

Core LLMs, Edge SLMs, Split Inference

We deploy large, general-purpose LLMs in central AI data centres, and smaller, domain-specific SLMs (including multilingual models) at the edge and on devices.

For a typical consumer query, an SLM at the edge handles intent detection, classification, language understanding and simple responses. Only when needed do we escalate to a larger LLM in the core for complex reasoning.

This split inference approach reduces backhaul, improves latency and cuts cost per token, while our orchestration layer decides dynamically which model path to use.

One AI Fabric for Jio and the Ecosystem

Internally, MyJio, JioTV+, JioCinema, JioMart and Jio Haptik agents connect to the Token Network via standard APIs and SDKs, so they can request tokens, select models and get usage stats programmatically.

Externally, partner apps and devices can integrate via developer APIs and edge gateways, enabling them to use Jio's token infrastructure for AI inference while we handle routing, metering and settlement.

This lets us support the user promise – "your Jio connection powers your AI everywhere" – with real, enforceable technical plumbing.

Commercial model

Designing a sustainable token business

Tokens extend Jio's core business model from GBs to AI compute. To make this sustainable, we need to balance user growth, ecosystem development and profitability with a clear token economics framework.

What determines token cost – and how we bend the curve

Token cost is driven by model choice, hardware efficiency, power pricing, location (core vs edge), and utilisation levels.

01

Capex Scale

Large AI-optimised data centres and long-term chip partnerships to lower compute cost per token.

02

Model Strategy

Aggressive use of SLMs, quantisation and fine-tuned open-source models to minimise per-token cost while keeping quality high.

03

Energy Strategy

Integrating renewables and smart cooling to stabilise long-term opex.

04

Network Placement

Placing workloads at the optimal point (edge vs core vs device) to reduce backhaul and improve utilisation.

From ARPU to ARPT: Average Revenue Per Token

At the consumer level, tokens surface as simple benefits, but internally we track ARPT (Average Revenue Per Token) as a new KPI alongside ARPU.

Bundled Value

AI tokens included in broadband/mobile plans, increasing stickiness, reducing churn and supporting tariff discipline.

Upsell

AI-heavy users (learners, professionals, creators) buying AI boosters or higher tiers.

Cross-sell

AI-powered features driving higher engagement and cross-usage of Jio's own services (content, commerce, finance).

Management can model:

  • Token cost curve vs data cost curve
  • Incremental ARPU uplift from AI capabilities (e.g., higher-value bundles, lower churn)
  • Payback period for AI infra capex under conservative and aggressive adoption scenarios

Jio's role is to provide the connectivity, AI infrastructure and token metering; government agencies define policy, content and outcomes.
